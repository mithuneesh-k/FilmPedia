Story

The Lego Batman Movie (2017) is a spin-off of The Lego Movie. It follows Batman as he faces his greatest fear: opening up to others. When the Joker threatens Gotham, Batman must learn teamwork and family values, especially with Robin and Barbara Gordon, while confronting his own insecurities and loneliness.

Reviews

  • Letterboxd: Averaging 3.6/5 stars, praised for its humor and meta references to Batman lore.
  • IMDb: Holds a 7.3/10 rating with over 150K votes, showing strong audience enjoyment.
  • Rotten Tomatoes: Critics gave it 90%, audiences 80%, making it one of the best-reviewed Batman films.
  • Metacritic: Scored 75 from critics, with users rating it around 7.8.
  • X (Twitter): Fans loved its comedic take on Batman, calling it “the funniest Batman movie ever.”

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: February 10, 2017 (US)
  • Runtime: 104 minutes
  • Budget: $80 million
  • Box Office: $312 million worldwide
  • Best enjoyed with family and friends for its humor and vibrant animation.
  • Features countless references to Batman’s cinematic and comic history.

Fun Facts

  • Will Arnett reprised his role as Lego Batman from *The Lego Movie* (2014).
  • The film pokes fun at nearly every Batman movie ever made.
  • Features cameos from villains across pop culture, including Voldemort and Sauron.
  • Chris McKay, the director, later went on to direct *Renfield* (2023).
  • The film’s humor and animation style made it a critical and commercial success.
