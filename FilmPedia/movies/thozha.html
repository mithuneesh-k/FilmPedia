Story

Thozha (2016) is the Tamil adaptation of the French film *The Intouchables*. It tells the story of Vikramadhitya, a wealthy man left paralyzed after an accident, and Seenu, a carefree young man hired as his caretaker. Their bond grows into a deep friendship, teaching both men about trust, joy, and living life to the fullest despite challenges.

Reviews

  • Rotten Tomatoes: Not officially rated, but critics highlighted its heartfelt performances and adaptation quality.

  • X (Twitter): Fans celebrated the chemistry between Karthi and Nagarjuna, calling it “uplifting and inspiring.”

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: March 25, 2016 (India)
  • Runtime: 158 minutes
  • Budget: ₹45 crore
  • Box Office: ₹112 crore worldwide
  • Best enjoyed with family for its mix of humor and heartfelt drama.
  • Available in both Tamil (*Thozha*) and Telugu (*Oopiri*).

Fun Facts

  • Adapted from the French classic *The Intouchables* (2011).
  • Nagarjuna’s performance as a quadriplegic was widely acclaimed for its authenticity.
  • Karthi’s comedic timing brought a refreshing balance to the emotional narrative.
  • The film was simultaneously shot in Tamil (*Thozha*) and Telugu (*Oopiri*).
  • Music composed by Gopi Sunder added to the film’s uplifting tone.
