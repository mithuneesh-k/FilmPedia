Jai Bhim (2021)

Legal Drama • Crime • Social Justice

IMDB: 8.7

A brave lawyer fights for justice when a poor tribal man is falsely accused of theft and disappears from police custody. Based on a true story from 1993, the film exposes systemic oppression and the power of the law.

Story

Sengeni and Rajakannu are a couple from the Irular tribe who live a peaceful life until Rajakannu is arrested for a crime he didn't commit. When he goes missing from the police station, Sengeni seeks the help of Advocate Chandru, a human rights lawyer. What follows is a grueling legal battle that challenges the corrupt machinery of the state and fights for the dignity of the marginalized.

Reviews

  • IMDb: Briefly became the #1 highest-rated film on IMDb globally, surpassing *The Godfather* with a 9.6 score at its peak.
  • Rotten Tomatoes: Currently holds a 100% Critics Score, praised for its "unflinching honesty" and Suriya’s powerful performance.
  • Letterboxd: 4.0/5. Users described it as "painful but necessary viewing" and a "masterpiece of social cinema."
  • The Oscars: Featured on the official Oscars YouTube channel in the "Scene at the Academy" segment.

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: November 2, 2021 (Amazon Prime Video)
  • Runtime: 164 minutes
  • Content Warning: Includes intense scenes of police brutality and custodial torture; recommended for mature audiences.
  • Historical Context: It is based on a real-life case handled by Justice K. Chandru in the Madras High Court.

Fun Facts

  • The real Justice K. Chandru handled over 96,000 cases in his career and never took a single penny from tribal clients.
  • Lijo Mol Jose, who played Sengeni, lived with Irular families for weeks to learn their lifestyle and body language.
  • The title "Jai Bhim" is a slogan used by followers of Dr. B. R. Ambedkar, the father of the Indian Constitution.
  • It was the most searched Indian film on Google in the year 2021.
  • Suriya chose to release the film on OTT to ensure it reached a massive global audience quickly.
