Story

Bruce Almighty (2003) follows Bruce Nolan, a frustrated TV reporter who blames God for his misfortunes. In response, God grants Bruce His powers to see if he can handle the responsibility. Bruce initially uses them for selfish gains but soon learns valuable lessons about love, humility, and the importance of free will.

Reviews

  • Letterboxd: Fans enjoyed Carrey’s comedic performance, averaging around 3.2/5 stars.
  • IMDb: Holds a 6.8/10 rating with over 430K votes, showing solid audience approval.
  • Rotten Tomatoes: Critics gave it 48%, but audiences were more favorable at 72%.
  • Metacritic: Scored 46 from critics, but users rated it higher at 7.0.
  • X (Twitter): Still remembered fondly, especially Morgan Freeman’s portrayal of God.

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: May 23, 2003 (US)
  • Runtime: 101 minutes
  • Budget: $81 million
  • Box Office: $484 million worldwide
  • Best enjoyed as a lighthearted comedy with spiritual undertones.
  • Spawned a spin-off, “Evan Almighty” (2007).

Fun Facts

  • Morgan Freeman’s portrayal of God became iconic and widely praised.
  • Jim Carrey improvised many of his comedic scenes.
  • The film was shot in Buffalo, New York, where Bruce works as a TV reporter.
  • It was one of the highest-grossing comedies of 2003.
  • Jennifer Aniston’s role was praised for grounding the film emotionally.
