  • Produced by Stuart Cornfeld, Ben Stiller, Samuel Goldwyn Jr.

Story

Walter Mitty is a negative assets manager at Life magazine who frequently daydreams of fantastic adventures. When the magazine prepares for its final print issue, a crucial negative for the cover photo goes missing. To find the elusive photographer Sean O'Connell, Walter must step out of his comfort zone and travel to Greenland, Iceland, and the Himalayas, finally living a life more daring than his dreams.

Reviews

  • IMDb: Rated 7.3/10, praised for its breathtaking cinematography and inspiring message.
  • Rotten Tomatoes: Holds a 52% Critic score but a much higher 71% Audience score.
  • Metacritic: Scored 54, reflecting mixed to positive critical reception.
  • Visual Mastery: Widely acclaimed for its stunning visuals and "feel-good" atmosphere.

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: December 25, 2013 (US)
  • Runtime: 114 minutes
  • Budget: $90 million
  • Box Office: $188.1 million worldwide
  • Perfect for anyone needing a boost of inspiration or "wanderlust."
  • Look out for the incredible soundtrack featuring José González.

Fun Facts

  • The film is based on James Thurber's 1939 short story of the same name.
  • Much of the film was shot on location in Iceland, showcasing its stunning landscapes.
  • Ben Stiller performed many of his own stunts, including the leap into the helicopter.
  • The "Life" magazine motto featured in the film was actually created for the movie.
