Story

Tangled (2010) is Disney’s modern retelling of the classic Rapunzel fairy tale. The film follows Rapunzel, a princess with magical hair that can heal and restore youth, who has been hidden away in a tower by Mother Gothel. When she meets Flynn Rider, a charming thief, she escapes her tower and embarks on an adventure to see the floating lanterns and discover her true identity.

Reviews

  • Letterboxd: Averaging 3.7/5 stars, praised for its humor and heartwarming story.
  • IMDb: Holds a 7.7/10 rating with over 500K votes, showing strong audience love.
  • Rotten Tomatoes: Critics gave it 89%, audiences 87%, making it one of Disney’s best modern animated films.
  • Metacritic: Scored 71 from critics, with users rating it around 8.0.
  • X (Twitter): Fans still celebrate its songs, especially “I See the Light,” and its vibrant animation style.

Watcher's Guide

  • Release Date: November 24, 2010 (US)
  • Runtime: 100 minutes
  • Budget: $260 million
  • Box Office: $592.4 million worldwide
  • Best enjoyed with family for its humor, music, and adventure.
  • Features memorable songs by Alan Menken and Glenn Slater.

Fun Facts

  • Tangled was Disney’s 50th animated feature film.
  • The film’s animation combined traditional hand-drawn techniques with CGI for a unique look.
  • “I See the Light” was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Original Song.
  • Rapunzel’s hair was animated with over 100,000 individual strands.
  • Originally titled “Rapunzel,” Disney changed it to “Tangled” to appeal to a broader audience.
